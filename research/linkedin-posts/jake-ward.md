## **Post 3 — 25% of AI answers from LinkedIn and Reddit**

That's nearly 1 in 4 citations from top LLMs. Wild.  

If you're not posting on these platforms, then someone else will be shaping the answers.  

Here's what you need to do:  

1. Post original content, not reshares. 95% of cited LinkedIn posts are original. Reshares barely register.
2. Write to answer, not to impress. 54-64% of cited posts are educational or advice-driven. AI surfaces the most helpful content, not the most polished.

## **Post 4 — Google gets as many visits as all platforms combined**

Google alone gets as many visits as every other “big platform” COMBINED.  

YouTube, ChatGPT, WhatsApp, TikTok, Wikipedia, X, Reddit, Instagram, Facebook, Yahoo, Yandex, Microsoft.  

All 13 combined pull in 86.9B visits.  

Google alone pulls in 85.8B.  

But the obvious takeaway here is the wrong one.  

While most see Google as the internet's front door, it's really the biggest middleman in history.  

Rand Fishkin explained it best:  

“Search, while it’s the largest category, is a *response* to influence created in other channels. It rarely creates demand, rather, search converts interest into answers, navigation, and clicks.”  

No social media presence means Google has no search demand to convert.  

No Google presence means any demand you create leaks out the bottom of the funnel.  

Search and social aren’t separate strategies. They’re channels within one demand strategy.  

It needs both to actually work.



## Post 5 — **LinkedIn AI SEO playbook**

11% of AI answers use LinkedIn content.  

That’s more than YouTube, Wikipedia, and every major news publisher combined.  

So I analysed 5M+ AI answers and 14M+ citations to uncover exactly how to use LinkedIn to show up in AI.  

Here's my LinkedIn AI SEO playbook:  

1. Write like you want to be quoted

AI doesn't JUST link your content, it repeats it.  

- Define key concepts clearly  
- State your main point in the first 2-3 lines  
- Use simple, precise language

1. Prioritise original thinking over reposts

95% of cited content is original.  

- Stop resharing  
- Start publishing your own POV, frameworks, insights

1. Go all-in on LinkedIn articles

They get 8x more citations than posts.  

- Structure like a blog (clear answer first)  
- Optimise for Google, not just LinkedIn

1. Hit the AI-friendly length

- Articles: 500-2,000 words  
- Posts: 50-300 words

1. Teach, don't sell

AI prefers content that teaches AND shows.  

- Break down how something works  
- Share lessons, processes and results  
- Add real examples, results, experiences  
- Make your content genuinely useful

1. Post consistently (this matters more than followers)

- 5+ posts per month is the sweet spot  
- Frequency beats waiting for the perfect post

1. Don't chase virality

Most cited posts have 15-25 reactions and almost no comments.  

Relevance beats reach.  

1. Use both company and personal accounts

- Company pages get cited (especially by Perplexity)  
- Individuals dominate ChatGPT and Google AI

1. Treat LinkedIn like an SEO channel

LinkedIn isn't just for engagement anymore.  

- Target real search-style questions  
- Think: "what would someone ask ChatGPT?"  
- Build content around that

You're not just building an audience on here, you're also training AI on how to talk about your brand.  

Write like it matters… because now it really does.
