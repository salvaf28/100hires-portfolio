## **Post 2 —** Reddit success shouldn't be measured like paid ads — a smarter framework

Here is the mistake I see B2B teams make over and over.  
  
They measure Reddit success like paid ads.  
  
Clicks. Leads. Immediate conversions.  
  
That is the wrong scoreboard.  
  
A smarter framework looks like this.  
  
1) Reach. Many Reddit users never log in. A post with 60 upvotes may have reached hundreds of silent readers.  
  
2) Sentiment. Are conversations about your brand becoming more constructive over time?  
  
3) Pipeline influence. Is traffic from Reddit reaching high-intent pages and assisting conversions?  
  
4) Intelligence. Are product and marketing teams mining threads for roadmap insights and objections?  
  
When a single complaint thread surfaces dozens of validated pain points, that is not a PR problem. It is product research delivered in real time.  
  
Reddit is not just a top-of-funnel channel. It is a feedback engine that compounds across departments.

## **Post 3 —** Reddit doesn't hate marketing, it hates lazy marketing

Reddit doesn’t hate marketing. It hates lazy marketing.  
  
What we’re seeing right now is a shift in how communities respond to brands that try to manufacture credibility. Moderators are coordinating. Users are documenting patterns. Entire subreddits are forming to track alleged spam behavior.  
  
This is Reddit’s immune system getting stronger.  
  
And here’s why that matters for marketers: Reddit dominates search results and heavily influences AI answers. That creates pressure to “show up” at any cost. But shortcuts backfire publicly and permanently.  
  
If you’re leading content or growth, audit what’s being done in your name.  
List every account posting on your behalf.  
Map which subreddits they engage in.  
Review how they handle negative threads.  
  
Reddit should be treated like PR. Every post is a public statement. Governance isn’t optional anymore, it’s strategy.
